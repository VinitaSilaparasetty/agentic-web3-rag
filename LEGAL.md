## Attribution and display policy

This tool returns excerpts from third-party Web3 documentation only where:

1. The domain has been explicitly consented to by its maintainer (see [CONSENT.md](CONSENT.md)).
2. The display policy selected by the maintainer allows it (`link-only`, `snippet`, or `fulltext`).

All results include a direct link to the original source URL and the project/organisation name as the source.

Unknown or non-consented domains are never ingested and return no results.

---

## robots.txt and machine-readable opt-out

The ingest pipeline respects:

- `robots.txt` — paths marked `Disallow` for the crawl user-agent are not fetched.
- `X-Robots-Tag: noai` and `X-Robots-Tag: noindex` HTTP response headers — pages with these headers are skipped.
- TDM opt-out declarations (e.g. `tdm-reservation: 1` per the W3C TDM Reservation Protocol draft) — domains with this header are treated as `deny` regardless of other consent.

This exceeds the minimum requirement of Art. 4 of the EU DSM Copyright Directive (2019/790), under which rights holders may opt out of Text and Data Mining. Our consent model requires explicit opt-**in** instead.

## Takedown and opt-out

If you are a rights holder and want your documentation removed from the index:

**Option 1 — If you previously consented via GitHub issue:**
Comment "REVOKE" on your original consent issue. All indexed content is removed within 48 hours.

**Option 2 — Direct takedown request:**
Email: **info@aevoxis.de**
Subject: `Takedown: [your domain]`
Include: the domain(s), example URLs, and a brief statement of your rights in the content.

We will acknowledge within **2 business days** and complete removal within **48 hours** of verification.

## EU AI Act — Transparency notice

The `/assist` API endpoint may use a third-party large language model (OpenAI GPT) to generate answers. Answers generated by this endpoint are AI-generated and are marked with `"ai_generated": true` in the response body and the `X-AI-Generated: true` HTTP header.

Integrators who expose `/assist` responses to end users must inform those users that the content is AI-generated, as required by Article 50 of the EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689).
